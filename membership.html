Membership essentials

Eligibility, documentation, and cooperative benefits.

Review the official criteria before submitting your form so approvals remain fast and transparent.

Eligibility for Membership

  • Age of Permanent Resident in the Jurisdiction Must be of good character not less than 18.
  • Should be engaged in a legitimate business, self-employed or industry within the jurisdiction or should have given reasonable grounds for starting a new business.
  • Should be a person who is a person who is a resident outside the area but who is engaged in a business or industry within the jurisdiction and two permanent residents of the area should be produced as security when taking a loan.
  • 50 shares must be purchased to obtain a permanent membership.
  • Should have maintained an account as a good customer of the society prior to applying for permanent membership.

Applying for Membership

  • Those who are eligible for membership should submit a duly completed form duly in two copies to the Board of Directors of the Society through the General Secretary of the Society.
  • Members can be appointed subject to confirmation by the General Assembly after considering the applications submitted to them by the Board of Directors.
  • A fee is charged for entry into the society and the member is obliged to pay the fee. The entry fee will not be refunded upon withdrawal from membership.

Other necessary documents

  • Proof of residency certified by the Grama Niladhari.
  • A photocopy of the National Identity Card.
  • A recent passport-sized photograph.

Benefits for members

  • Automatic enrollment into the Welfare Fund.
  • Automatic enrollment into the Distress Relief Fund.
  • Continued access to dividends, concessional lending, and cooperative advisory support.
